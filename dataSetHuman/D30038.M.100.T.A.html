[1] In December 1998 a story broke [2] that relatives of International Olympic Committee ( IOC ) [3] members had received $ 400,000 in scholarships from promoters of Salt Lake City for the 2002 Winter Olympic Games . [4] The IOC immediately ordered a top-level investigation . [5] Senior IOC official Marc Hodler called the scholarships a `` bribe . '' [6] The IOC rules stated that cities could not give any IOC member anything exceeding $ 150 in value . [7] Hodler went on to allege similar malpractice in choosing other Olympic sites [8] and estimated that 5-7 % of the 115 IOC members received compensation for their votes .
