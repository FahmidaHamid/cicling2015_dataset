[1] China continues to arrest dissidents [2] even as it prepares to sign a UN human rights treaty [3] and to host the first visit by a British PM in seven years . [4] The dissident China Democracy Party believes [5] it is legal to challenge the communists ' power monopoly , but the PRC [6] cites a `` grab-bag '' section of the criminal code [7] making almost any political activity illegal . [8] The US has protested the arrests . [9] Two dissidents , Qin Yongmin and Wang Youcai must defend themselves at trail [10] since lawyers have been dissuaded from assisting them . [11] Other dissidents continue to be harassed and arrested . [12] One dissident , Yao Zhenxian , was released from a labor camp
