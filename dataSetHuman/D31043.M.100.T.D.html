[1] Power in Lebanon is shared equally by a Maronite Christian president , a Sunni Muslim prime minister , and a Shiite Parliament speaker , [2] an arrangement made to prevent a recurrence of the 1975-90 civil war . [3] Syria , with 30,000 troops in Lebanon is the main power broker there . [4] The Lebanese parliament amended the constitution [5] to permit popular army general Emile Lahoud to become president . [6] Prime minister Rafik Hariri , the architect of Lebanon 's postwar reconstruction , expected to get a fourth term but a conflict [7] with the new president led him to bow out as premier . [8] Lebanon 's economic stability has been threatened by the conflict .
