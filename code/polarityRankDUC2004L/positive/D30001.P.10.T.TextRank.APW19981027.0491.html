Events mentioned in the resolution include Hun Sen's coup last year against his co-prime minister, Prince Norodom Ranariddh, and his violent crackdown in September against anti-government demonstrations. [2] A copy of the resolution has since been submitted to the U.S. Senate Committee on Foreign Relations. [4] It also repeated claims that the prince's party had moved rebel soldiers into Phnom Penh, a contention that remains unproved. [5] Ranariddh and his core supporters did not return to Cambodia until a few months before an election in July this year that the ruling party narrowly won. [6] The Khmer Rouge was responsible for the deaths of as many as 2 million people during the guerrilla group's 1975-79 rule of Cambodia.
