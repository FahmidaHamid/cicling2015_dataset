[1] Hun Sen and Ranariddh, co-prime ministers in a coalition formed in 1993 after a landmark U.N.-sponsored election, often clashed over power-sharing and the integration of guerrilla fighters from the crumbling Khmer Rouge. [2] Their arguments turned bloody last year when Hun Sen ousted Ranariddh in a coup. [3] The prince fled Cambodia and did not return until a few months before elections in July. [4] Hun Sen's Cambodian People's Party narrowly won the polls, but a strong second-place finish by Ranariddh's FUNCINPEC gave the royalist party leverage in post-election negotiations. [5] After a three-month impasse, they agreed last week to a coalition deal that will make Hun Sen sole prime minister and Ranariddh president of the National Assembly. [6] "This will not be a repetition of 1993 because there will not be two prime ministers," said Hun Sen aide Prak Sokhon, who attended last week's summit. [7] "When we had two prime ministers, everything moved very slowly and decisions were made more politically than technically".
