[1] Duisenberg was named this spring as head of the new European Central Bank, which will govern the policies of the euro, the new single currency which goes into effect Jan. 1. [2] Duisenberg told Le Monde, a newspaper that fervently has backed the euro, that the the introduction of the new currency is the most "significant" step so far toward European unification. [3] However, he said that he would like to see further political integration, especially in the area of foreign tax and social policies.
